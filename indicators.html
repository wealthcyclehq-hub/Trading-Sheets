The 3 Key Indicators
MA
Moving Averages
Average price over a set number of days
A moving average smooths out daily noise and shows the overall direction the stock is heading. Look at where the stock is relative to the line.
Weather Analogy
Instead of caring about Tuesday's exact temperature, you look at the 30-day average to know if seasons are changing. MAs do the same for price.
50-day MA — Medium trend
200-day MA — Long trend
VWAP
VWAP
Volume Weighted Average Price — resets daily
The average price everyone has paid today, weighted by how many shares traded at each level. Resets every market open.

Support & Resistance — Price Floors and Ceilings
These are invisible price levels where buyers and sellers have historically battled. Stocks tend to bounce at support and stall at resistance.
🔑 When the stock breaks through resistance with high volume — that old ceiling often becomes the new floor. This is called a breakout, and it often signals a strong move higher.
Basic Patterns — Tap Each to Expand
Bullish Setup
Breakout Pattern
When price pushes above a resistance level it has tested multiple times — often signals a strong move upward.
How to spot it: Stock hits the same ceiling 2–3 times. Then volume surges as price closes above it. That's your breakout signal.

Key rule: Low-volume breakouts are often false — always check that volume increases to confirm.

Opportunity Setup
Pullback Pattern
After rising, the stock temporarily drops before continuing upward. Pullbacks are normal — and often excellent entry points.
How to spot it: Uptrending stock drops 5–10% briefly. RSI may dip. Price may touch the moving average before bouncing back.

Beginner tip: Don't panic when you see this in a stock you own. It's often the stock "catching its breath" before continuing higher.

How Beginners Combine Indicators
1
MA + Support: Stock is above the 50-day MA and bouncing off a support level → potential setup worth watching
2
RSI + VWAP: RSI under 35 (oversold) and stock trading above VWAP → possible bounce opportunity with two confirmations
3
Breakout + Volume spike: Price closes above resistance and volume surges → stronger confirmation that the move is real
Golden Rule: Use 2–3 indicators maximum. More indicators means more noise — not more accuracy.

Common Mistakes
✕
Using 5+ indicators at once — this creates "analysis paralysis"
✕
Treating RSI 70+ as an automatic sell — trends can stay overbought
✕
Ignoring volume — it validates or cancels every signal
✕
Only looking at short-term charts — zoom out for context
✕
Basing a full trade decision on one indicator alone
Key Takeaways
MA shows trend direction — stock above line = uptrend in play
VWAP = daily fair value. Above it signals buyer strength.
RSI below 30 = possible buy zone. Above 70 = caution zone.
Support = floor. Resistance = ceiling. Breakouts = opportunity.
Use 2–3 indicators max — always confirm signals with volume
