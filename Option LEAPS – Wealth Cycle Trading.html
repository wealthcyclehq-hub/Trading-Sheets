What Are LEAPS? — Three Reasons They Work
More Time
Expirations 12–36 months away. You aren't rushed — the trade has room to breathe and develop.
Lower Capital
Control 100 shares for a fraction of the full cost. A $5,000 stock position might cost $700 as a LEAP.
Amplified Gain
If the stock rises strongly, your LEAP can multiply 3–5x in value while you risked only the premium.
Control Without Full Ownership
A LEAP lets you benefit from 100 shares of a company without paying full market price for every share.
Real Estate Analogy
A $400K house deposit ($10K) secures your right to buy it. You benefit if the value rises — without paying the full price yet.
Less Pressure Than Short Options
Regular options expire in days or weeks — that's stressful. LEAPS give you months or years to be right.
Gardening Analogy
A regular option is planting a seed and demanding harvest in 7 days. A LEAP is planting in spring and waiting until fall. The crop has time to grow.

Safer Choice
Conservative LEAP
◆Expiration: 1.5–3 years out
◆Strike: At or below current price (ITM)
◆Higher premium, higher reliability
ITM LEAPS already have built-in value, behave more like the stock itself (high delta), and decay slowly. Best for beginners starting out. Think of it as the "no-frills, solid foundation" pick.

Riskier Choice
Aggressive LEAP
◆Expiration: 6–12 months
◆Strike: Above current price (OTM)
◆Cheaper, but needs a big stock move
OTM LEAPS are cheaper but require the stock to move significantly in your favor. High reward potential — but could expire worthless. Not recommended until you're comfortable with how LEAPS work.

Simple Example — Full Walkthrough
1
You believe Company ABC will grow over 2 years. It currently trades at $50/share
2
Buying 100 shares outright would cost $5,000 — a significant commitment
3
Instead, you buy a Call LEAP: $50 strike price, 2-year expiration — costs $700 premium
4
18 months later, Company ABC rises to $90/share — a $40 increase
5
Your LEAP is now worth ~$4,200. You sell it before expiration
You risked $700 instead of $5,000
+$3,500 profit (5× return)
The Secret Ingredient: Patience
LEAPS reward waiting. You don't need to watch the stock daily. Plant the position, review it quarterly, and let time do the work. Most LEAP profits arrive in months 8–18, not week one.
Pro Tips
✓
Choose companies you genuinely believe in long-term
✓
Pick ITM or ATM strikes — OTM is too risky to start
✓
Target at least 18 months of expiration time
✓
Sell before expiration — don't let a winning LEAP expire
✓
Set a quarterly calendar reminder to review your position
Common Mistakes
✕
Picking expirations under 6 months — defeats the LEAP purpose
✕
Buying far OTM as a beginner — high chance of total loss
✕
Panic selling during a normal market dip mid-LEAP
✕
Forgetting you hold it — options require active management
✕
Concentrating all capital into one LEAP on one company
Key Takeaways
LEAPS = options with 1–3 year expirations — same mechanics, more time
Control 100 shares for a fraction of the full stock cost
ITM + 2-year expiration = the safest LEAP combination for beginners
Time decay (Theta) is slow on LEAPS — you won't lose value overnight
Patience is your edge — most LEAP profits come after month 6
