Stock Market
Basics

What a stock is, how the market works, and how ordinary people grow wealth over time — explained plainly.

Quick Definitions — tap each to expand
Stock
A small ownership piece of a company.
Buying stock = buying a tiny slice of that business. If the company grows, your slice is worth more.

How the Market Works
Companies sell shares to raise money. Anyone can then buy and sell those shares on an exchange — like the NYSE or Nasdaq.
Analogy
Think of a farmers market. Sellers list produce; buyers pick what they want. Popular items rise in price. Unpopular ones drop. Stocks work the same way — demand drives price.
  • 1Company lists shares to raise money
  • 2Investors buy those shares through a broker
  • 3Prices shift daily based on supply and demand
How People Make Money
The core idea: buy low, sell high. You also earn from dividends — cash companies pay just for holding their stock.
Analogy
You buy limited sneakers for $100. Demand surges. You sell for $180. That $80 is your profit. Stocks work identically — the "sneaker" is a company share.
Types of Stocks — Tap Each to Learn More
Different stocks have different risk and reward profiles. Two types beginners should know first:
Blue Chip Stocks
Large, established companies (Apple, Coca-Cola, Microsoft). Stable, reliable, often pay dividends.
These are the "blue-ribbon" companies of the stock world. They've survived recessions, they pay dividends regularly, and they don't swing wildly. Perfect starting point for beginners. Lower reward — but far lower risk.
Growth Stocks
Younger companies expanding fast. Higher potential gain — but prices swing more dramatically.
Like backing a new restaurant getting rave reviews. Could explode in value — or stumble badly. These suit investors who can handle volatility and have a longer time horizon. Not ideal as your first and only pick.

6 months later — stock rises to $35/share Value: $175

Pro Tips
✓
Start with as little as $10 — the habit matters more than the amount
✓
Spread across multiple companies, never bet everything on one
✓
Check if the company is actually profitable before buying
✓
Long-term investors almost always outperform short-term traders
✓
Use apps like Fidelity or Schwab — both free to open
Common Mistakes
✕
Panic selling when prices dip temporarily
✕
Buying based on social media hype alone
✕
Investing money you need in the next 6 months
✕
Refreshing your portfolio every hour — it changes nothing
✕
Expecting overnight wealth — this takes time
Key Takeaways
A stock = owning a real piece of a real company
Buy low, sell high — or hold long for compounding growth
Start small, diversify early, and never risk money you need
Blue chips = stable. Growth stocks = higher risk, higher reward.
Patience is the most underrated edge in investing
